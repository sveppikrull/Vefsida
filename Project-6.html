Project 6 - Final Project

Project description

In this final project we will be planning a build of an object that consists of both CNC milling out of wood and the use of a resin filled silicone mold. The group consists of Arnór Breki Ásþórsson and Sverrir Kristinsson. On this page we will be documenting the project which consists of:

1. Laying out the plan of the build process.

2. Divide workload among group members.

3. Determining an intellectual property licence for the object.

4. Making a single sheet publication.

5. Making a presentation video.

Workload division

Arnór Breki Ásþórsson: Design and documentation of bookshelf. Single sheet publictaion, project managment and video.

Sverrir Kristinsson: Design and documentation of headphone hook. Documentation of final project.

Both members: Manufacturing of bookshelf.

Inspiration

For this project we wanted to build something functional. And after a good brainstorming session we decided to make a bookshelf out of wood. The idea for the general design of the bookshelf came from this website. To go with the bookshelf we decided to make a hook to hold a pair of headphones.

Project Management

In the first meeting we started to brainstorm for ideas, in the second meeting we decided on an idea and divided the responsibilities between us. As stated before, Arnór would design the bookshelf and I would design the headphone hook. A detailed layout of the project management can be seen here below.

Intellectual property licence

Since we had no intentions on selling the design and wanted to make sure that anyone could access and use our design free of charge we decided to use the “MIT licence”. The MIT license, sometimes referred to as the Expat licence is a permissive free software license from MIT(Massachusetts Institute of Technology). Since the licence puts very limited restrictions on what can be done with what the licence protects it has a high licence capacity. Further information on the MIT licence can be found on its wikipedia page.
